Question Builder (anti-fautes) 🎯

Compose une question correcte en choisissant les éléments. Le Builder gère l’auxiliaire, l’ordre et le temps.

Rappels : verbe be → pas de “do/does/did”. Present perfect = have/has + V-en. Présent continu = be + V-ing.

Grammaire — fiches claires

Former des questions (ordre & auxiliaires)

  • Do/Does + sujet + base (présent) : Do you like coffee? / Does she work?
  • Did + sujet + base (passé) : Did you go yesterday?
  • Be + sujet + V-ing : Are you working now?
  • Have/Has + sujet + V-en : Have you finished?
  • Wh-word + auxiliaire + sujet + verbe : Where do you live?
⚠️ Avec be : Where are you? (pas *do you are*)

Temps — quand les utiliser

  • Present Simple : habitudes / vérités — I work on Mondays.
  • Present Continuous : maintenant / plan — I’m cooking / I’m meeting her at 8.
  • Past Simple : action finie — We visited Rome last year.
  • Will : décision instantanée / promesse — I’ll call you.
  • Be going to : intention / signe — I’m going to cook pasta.
  • Present Perfect : expérience / durée — I’ve lived here for 3 years.
Erreurs fréquentes → versions correctes
  • *How long do you living here?* → ✅ How long have you been living here?
  • *What time lives your train?* → ✅ What time does your train leave?
  • *Who have cooked the evening?* → ✅ Who cooked dinner? / Who has cooked dinner?
  • *How many for this T-shirt?* → ✅ How much is this T-shirt?

Exercices — auto-correction

1) Choix multiples (auxiliaires & temps)

  1. How long ___ you been living here? you been living here?
  2. What time ___ your train ___ ? (today)
  3. Why ___ you tired today?

2) Traduction ciblée

“Tu as un joli sourire.” →

“Depuis combien de temps attends-tu le bus ?” →

3) Connecteurs (so / because / however / therefore)

I was tired, I went home.

I stayed home it was raining.

It was closed; we left.

4) Ordre des mots (question → phrase correcte)

Exemple : *How long do you living here?* → How long have you been living here?

Prononciation express (simplifiée)

  • th : langue entre les dents → this (zis), think (ssink doux).
  • h aspiré : happy (ha-pi), house (haous).
  • r anglais léger : girl (gueurl), tomorrow (te-mo-ro).
  • Accent tonique : beau-TI-ful (biou-TI-feul), to-DAY (te-DÉ).
  • beach = bii-tch (voyelle longue) — pas “bitch”.
